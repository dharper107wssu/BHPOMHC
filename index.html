Central Operating Principle: No system operates in isolation. The prescriber's note affects billing. The employment goal affects medication timing. The ACTT field visit generates a claim. Every clinical decision has a documentation consequence, and every documentation gap creates a compliance risk. Design your workflows so these connections are explicit — never assumed.

Psychological Testing Systems

Testing in an OMHC feeds treatment planning, prescribing, and psychotherapy. It is not a standalone referral — it must be integrated into the care team's workflow.

Maryland Law: Psychological testing may only be administered, scored, and interpreted by a Licensed Psychologist (PhD/PsyD) or under their direct supervision. This is a scope-of-practice boundary that must appear in your P&P, credentialing files, and supervision agreements.

Testing Referral & Workflow — 7 Steps

1

Clinical Indication & Referral

Intake clinician or treating therapist identifies need. Clinical question specified: diagnostic clarification, treatment planning, disability documentation, or neuropsychological concerns.

LCSW-C / Psychiatrist / LCSWPrior Auth Required — Obtain BEFORE Scheduling
2

Pre-Testing Review (Psychologist)

Licensed Psychologist reviews referral question, prior records, diagnoses, and medications. Selects battery: cognitive (WAIS-IV), personality/diagnostic (MMPI-3, PAI), achievement (WIAT-4), neuropsychological, or trauma-focused.

Licensed Psychologist Only
3

Administration

Administered by Licensed Psychologist or supervised Psychologist Assistant. Standardized conditions. Accessibility accommodations documented. Supervision of PA documented in chart.

PhD/PsyD or Supervised PA
4

Scoring, Interpretation & Report

Licensed Psychologist interprets all results. Report: referral question, history, instruments, behavioral observations, domain results, integrated interpretation, diagnostic formulation, specific clinical recommendations.

Psychologist Only — Cannot DelegateWithin 14 Business Days
5

Feedback Session with Client

Psychologist reviews results with client in accessible language. Family/supports included with consent. Separately billable service.

Separately Billable
6

Team Distribution & ITP Update

Report to: referring clinician, prescriber, ACTT team lead. ITP updated within 10 days. All receiving clinicians document receipt in chart.

Full Team NotificationITP Revised Within 10 Days
7

Records, Security & Re-evaluation

Test protocols retained separately from medical record with restricted access. Psychologist controls access. Raw data released only to licensed psychologists (APA ethics + Maryland law). Re-evaluation typically 2–3 years for cognitive.

Critical OMHC & ACHC Requirement: Clinical supervision is a regulated activity in Maryland. You must have written supervision agreements, documented supervision logs with signatures, and supervision session notes. ACHC and OHCQ will review supervision documentation during surveys. Supervision by an unqualified supervisor creates licensure jeopardy for the supervisee.

Supervision Infrastructure to Build

Documentation Requirements
Written supervision agreement for every supervisee: goals, frequency, format, evaluation criteria, emergency coverage path
Supervision log: date, duration, cases discussed, supervisee signature, supervisor signature — in personnel AND supervision file
Individual supervision minimum 1 hour/week for pre-licensed staff; group supervision additional — not a substitute
Co-signature in EHR: supervisor co-signs within 72 hours; billing held until co-signed
Supervisor credentialing: document Board approval to supervise (Boards of Social Work, Counselors, MFT Examiners)
Therapy Service Workflows
Individual therapy: note documents progress toward ITP goals, not just session content summary
Group therapy: group note + individual participation note per client; group curriculum on file; payer group size limits
Family therapy: all participants documented; adult consent obtained; billing code differs from individual
Telehealth: Maryland originating site documentation; separate telehealth consent; audio-only policy separate from video
After-hours: pre-licensed clinicians must have a clear documented path to reach supervisor for urgent clinical questions

ACTT Program Setup

Maryland ACTT requires BHA program approval before billing. Complies with COMAR 10.21.17. Contact BHTAC (free) before building your program.

First Step: Contact Maryland BHA's Behavioral Health Technical Assistance Center (BHTAC) before building your ACTT program. They provide free consultation on approval, fidelity, and startup. ACTT programs must be licensed through OHCQ under COMAR 10.21.17 and receive BHA program approval before billing ACTT-specific codes.
